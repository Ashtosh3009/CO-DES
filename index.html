Different Exams based on Ages

This site also provides kids, teenagers and senior citizens.

Kids (ages 5-11)

Children in this age group may take exams that assess their basic skills, such as reading, writing, and arithmetic. Examples of exams for this age group include the SATs (Standard Assessment Tests) in the UK, and the MAP (Measures of Academic Progress) test in the US.

Teenagers (ages 12-18)

Teenagers may take exams that assess their subject-specific knowledge and skills, such as the GCSE (General Certificate of Secondary Education) in the UK, or the Advanced Placement (AP) exams in the US. Additionally, students in this age group may take college entrance exams, such as the SAT or ACT in the US.

Senior students (ages 18 and above)

Senior students may take exams that assess their knowledge and skills in specific fields, such as the LSAT (Law School Admission Test) for those seeking to enter law school, or the MCAT (Medical College Admission Test) for those seeking to enter medical school. Additionally, students in this age group may take professional certification exams, such as the CPA (Certified Public Accountant) exam for those seeking to become accountants.
